Project: The Modernist Retreat

Project Overview

The Modernist Retreat is a residential project that embodies minimalist design principles while integrating sustainable engineering solutions. Located in a serene landscape, the house features clean lines, open spaces, and a seamless blend of indoor and outdoor living. The design emphasizes natural light, energy efficiency, and the use of eco-friendly materials.

Role

As the lead architectural engineer, I was responsible for the structural design, energy modeling, and sustainable systems integration. My role involved ensuring the structural integrity of the design while optimizing energy performance and minimizing environmental impact.

Key Challenges & Solutions

One of the main challenges was to achieve the desired open-plan layout while maintaining structural stability. This was addressed through the use of advanced structural analysis and innovative engineering techniques, such as a concealed steel frame and cantilevered elements. Another challenge was to maximize energy efficiency. Solutions included high-performance insulation, a geothermal heating and cooling system, and solar panels integrated into the roof design. The selection of sustainable materials, such as reclaimed wood and recycled concrete, further contributed to the project's environmental goals.
