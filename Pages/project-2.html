Project: The Green Haven

A sustainable residential complex in the heart of the city.

Project Overview

The Green Haven is a residential complex designed with sustainability at its core. The project integrates various green building technologies to minimize environmental impact while providing a comfortable living space for residents. The complex features a green roof, solar panels, and a rainwater harvesting system.

Building Systems

Structural System

Steel frame with concrete core

HVAC System

High-efficiency VRF system

Electrical System

Smart lighting and energy management

Plumbing System

Low-flow fixtures and greywater recycling

Contributions

As the lead engineer on this project, I was responsible for the design and coordination of all building systems. My key contributions included optimizing the structural design for material efficiency, selecting and integrating the HVAC system for maximum energy savings, and ensuring seamless coordination between all engineering disciplines.

Innovative Solutions

One of the innovative solutions implemented in The Green Haven is the integration of a greywater recycling system. This system collects and treats wastewater from showers and sinks for reuse in toilet flushing and irrigation, significantly reducing water consumption. Additionally, the smart lighting system uses occupancy sensors and daylight harvesting to minimize energy use while maintaining optimal lighting levels.
